# Medical Malpractice Exposure Review (West Africa Telehealth)

**Date**: 2025-09-14  
**Prepared by**: Legal Research (NaijaCare)

## Summary
Telehealth liability precedents in West Africa indicate heightened exposure when
remote services are used without documented clinical oversight. Insurers require
explicit documentation of on-call clinician coverage and incident response.

## Findings
- Nigerian malpractice coverage for telehealth mandates named physician
  accountability.
- Claims history indicates higher settlement risk when automated triage was
  involved in patient escalation.
- Insurers require documented escalation protocols and audit trails.

## Requirements
- Implement clinician on-call policies and audit logs for routing decisions.
- Ensure each case has a responsible clinician of record.
- Maintain immutable audit trails for at least seven years.
